\begin{exercisebox}{Exercise 5}
Let $A$ be a nonempty set of real numbers which is bounded below. Let $-A$ be the set of all numbers $-x$, where $x \in A$. Prove that
\[
\inf A = -\sup(-A).
\]
\end{exercisebox}

\textbf{Solution}

Suppose $\inf A = \beta$ exists (proposition).

Let $x \in A$. Then $\beta \leq x$, so we have $-x \leq -\beta$ (Note that $-x \in -A$). Then $-\beta$ is an upper bound of $-A$.

Let $y$ be such that $y < -\beta$, then $-y > \beta$, then $-y$ is not a lower bound of $A$.

Then there exists an $\hat{x} \in A$ such that $-y > \hat{x}$, then $-\hat{x} > y$ (Note that $-\hat{x} \in -A$) , then $y$ is not an upper bound of $-A$.

Therefore $-\inf A = \sup(-A)$, which implies $\inf A = -\sup(-A)$. $\blacksquare$

\begin{exercisebox}{Proposition (Proved on the book)}
Suppose $S$ is an ordered set with the least-upper-bound property, $B \subset S$, $B$ is not empty, and $B$ is bounded below. Let $L$ be the set of all lower bounds of $B$. Then
\[ \alpha = \sup L \]
exists in $S$, and $\alpha = \inf B$.
\par \quad In particular, $\inf B$ exists in $S$.
\end{exercisebox}
