\begin{exercisebox}{Probability Exercise}
Let $X$ be a random variable such that $X \sim Bin(n, p)$. If $p(x)$ represents the p.m.f. of $X$, show that:

$$
p(x + 1) = K p(x) ,
$$

where $K$ depends on $x$ and $n$. Find $K$. Justify your answer.
\end{exercisebox}

\textbf{Solution}

Note that

\[
\begin{aligned}
    p(x+1) &=\binom{n}{x+1} \cdot p^{x+1} \cdot (1 - p)^{n - (x+1)}\\
    &= \dfrac{(n-x)}{(x+1)} \cdot \dfrac{p}{(1-p)} \cdot \binom{n}{x}\cdot p^{x}\cdot (1 - p)^{n - x}\\
    &= \dfrac{(n-x)}{(x+1)} \cdot \dfrac{p}{(1-p)} \cdot p(x)
\end{aligned}
\]

Therefore, $K(x,n) = \dfrac{(n-x)}{(x+1)} \cdot \dfrac{p}{(1-p)}$.
